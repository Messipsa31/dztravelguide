01 - Adrar

Adrar Province is located in southwestern Algeria and is one of the largest provinces by area. It is characterized by its rich Saharan character, featuring oases and traditional "Ksour" (palaces) that reflect an ancient architectural heritage. The region is famous for its diverse culture that blends Tuareg and Arab traditions, making it an important tourist destination for lovers of tranquility and desert nature.

Top tourist places in Adrar:

  • Timimoun Palace: A beautiful oasis surrounded by sand dunes, famous for its traditional red buildings.
  • Aoulef Palace: One of the oldest historical palaces in the region, containing archaeological mosques and traditional markets.
  • Fenoughil Oasis: A charming natural spot featuring palm groves and springs.
  • Tinerkouk Desert: One of the most beautiful Saharan areas, offering breathtaking and peaceful views.

02 - Chlef

Chlef Province is located in northern Algeria and is distinguished by its location between plains and mountains, giving it a unique natural diversity. The province is known for its green nature and diverse terrain; it is also an important agricultural region in the country, as well as being a promising tourist destination.

Top tourist places in Chlef:

  • Dahra Mountains: A stunning mountain range ideal for nature enthusiasts and hiking.
  • Oued El Kasseb Forest: A vast green area that attracts visitors from everywhere, especially during the spring.
  • Hammam Sidi Okacha Mineral Waters: Known for their therapeutic benefits, attracting visitors for relaxation.
  • Ténès Coast: Stretching along the Mediterranean Sea, it features beautiful beaches and historical landmarks like the Casbah of Ténès.

03 - Laghouat

Laghouat Province is located in central Algeria and is considered the gateway to the Sahara Desert. It is characterized by its charming Saharan nature, blending ancient heritage with stunning landscapes. It is also one of the oldest Algerian cities, famous for its long history of resistance against French colonialism, as well as its traditional architecture and customs.

Top tourist places in Laghouat:

  • The Old City: Featuring narrow alleys, mosques, and traditional buildings dating back centuries.
  • Oases and Palms: Scattered around the city, forming charming natural landscapes and a source of fruits and dates.
  • Beni Hilal Palace: A historical palace from the Ottoman era.
  • Mount Tadjemout: An attraction for nature and camping enthusiasts, overlooking magnificent panoramic views.

04 - Oum El Bouaghi

Oum El Bouaghi Province is located in eastern Algeria and is characterized by its agricultural and civilized character. It is also considered one of the regions rich in historical and archaeological heritage. The city is famous for its natural beauty and diverse terrain of plains and mountains, in addition to its ancient archaeological sites.

Top tourist places in Oum El Bouaghi:

  • Tifache Archaeological Site: An ancient Roman city containing historical theaters and columns.
  • Ain Zitoun Forest: A wonderful place for walking and relaxation in the heart of nature.
  • Mineral Springs: Popular resorts visited by tourists for healing and relaxation.
  • Boussouf Palace: A historical landmark reflecting the traditional architecture of the region.

05 - Batna

Batna Province is located in the heart of the Aurès in eastern Algeria. It is considered one of the most important historical and tourist provinces, distinguished by its mountainous beauty and ancient heritage. It is also known as the cradle of the free Amazigh and the home of the hero Shoshenq.

Top tourist places in Batna:

  • Timgad: A Roman archaeological city classified as a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring a huge theater and straight streets.
  • Belezma: A towering mountain offering breathtaking views and great opportunities for adventure lovers.
  • Medracen Tomb: The oldest funeral monument in North Africa, with a unique circular architectural shape.
  • Kimmel Forest: A quiet natural haven for relaxation enthusiasts.
  • Traditional Aurès Market: Displays local handicrafts that express the identity of the region.

06 - Béjaïa

Béjaïa is considered one of the most beautiful coastal provinces in Algeria. Located in the Little Kabylie region, it is distinguished by its stunning combination of mountains and sea, making it a prime tourist destination. Béjaïa is also known for its ancient history and rich Amazigh culture.

Top Tourist Attractions in Béjaïa:

  • Mount Gouraya: Offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea, especially at sunset.
  • Cap Carbon: Home to one of the highest maritime cliffs in the Mediterranean.
  • Aokas Beach: A serene and charming beach characterized by its turquoise waters and golden sands.
  • Kefrida Waterfalls: A magnificent natural waterfall surrounded by dense forest, an ideal destination for nature lovers.
  • The Old Kasbah: Narrow alleys and historical architecture reflecting Amazigh and Ottoman styles.
  • Aokas Cave: A stunning natural cave that is a favorite destination for exploration and adventure enthusiasts.
  • Tichy Beach: One of the most famous beaches in the province, attracting visitors from various regions.
  • Gouraya National Park: A nature reserve home to a unique biological diversity of flora and fauna.
  • Sidi Touati Shrine: An important religious and historical landmark visited by many locals and tourists.
  • The Old Port: An ideal spot for strolling and enjoying the city's seaside atmosphere.

07 - Biskra

Biskra is known as the "Gateway to the Desert" due to its geographical location that bridges the mountains and the Sahara. It features a warm winter climate and is an important agricultural and tourism hub, especially famous for the worldwide production of "Deglet Nour" dates.

Best Tourist Places in Biskra:

  • Wadi El Hamma: A beautiful oasis and a great site for swimming and relaxation amidst nature.
  • M’chouneche Waterfalls: Some of the most beautiful waterfalls in Algeria, located near the Tolga district.
  • Tolga Municipality: Famous for its dense palm groves and premium date products.
  • Southern Aurès Mountains: Perfect for mountain trekking and adventure lovers.
  • Thermal Springs (Hammam): Its sulfurous waters attract visitors for therapeutic healing and relaxation.
  • Old Palaces (Ksours) of Biskra: Telling the city's ancient history through traditional architectural styles.

08 - Béchar

Located in southwestern Algeria, Béchar Province serves as a gateway to the Sahara Desert. It is characterized by its authentic desert character and geographic diversity, ranging from oases and mountains to sand dunes, making it an attractive destination for desert and cultural tourism.

Best Tourist Places in Béchar:

  • Taghit: A magical tourist town nestled among the dunes, famous for its natural beauty and Saharan tranquility.
  • Beni Abbès: Known as the "Pearl of the Sahara," featuring a stunning oasis and magnificent traditional buildings.
  • Ancient Palaces (Ksours): Built from mud and stone, telling the history of the region's early inhabitants.
  • Golden Sand Dunes: Ideal for sandboarding and camping under the stars.
  • Timgarine Archaeological Rock Art: Dating back thousands of years, these are among the most important historical landmarks in the region.

09 - Blida

Known as the "City of Roses," Blida Province is located in northern Algeria, about 45 km from the capital. It combines breathtaking nature, mountains, forests, and springs, making it one of the most beautiful Algerian provinces and a top attraction for family and nature tourism.

Best Tourist Places in Blida:

  • Chréa National Park: One of Algeria's largest nature reserves, offering stunning views and a favorite destination for hiking and winter skiing.
  • Sidi El Kebir Shrine: A religious and tourist site featuring a panoramic view of the city and forests.
  • Wadi Beni Azza: An ideal place for trekking among forests and small waterfalls.
  • Blida City Center: Characterized by Ottoman architecture, traditional markets, and rose gardens.
  • Hammam Melouane: A destination for those seeking natural thermal healing and relaxation.

10 - Bouira

Located in northern Algeria, Bouira Province serves as a bridge between the High Plateaus and the Kabylie region. It is distinguished by its stunning nature, mountainous terrain, and moderate climate, making it a wonderful destination for nature lovers and mountain adventures.

Best Tourist Places in Bouira:

  • Djurdjura Mountains: One of the most beautiful mountain ranges in Algeria, famous for snowy landscapes in winter and green valleys in summer.
  • Tikjda: A famous mountain resort for skiing and camping, featuring breathtaking panoramic views and recreational activities.
  • Agoulmim Lake: Located near Tikjda, it is considered one of the most beautiful mountain lakes in Algeria.
  • Akouker Cave: A natural cave in the region's mountains, regarded as a fascinating site for exploration enthusiasts.
  • Gorges de Lakhdaria: A magnificent place for family trips and outings amidst nature.

11 - Tamanrasset

Tamanrasset is one of the largest Algerian provinces by area, located in the far south. This province is distinguished by its breathtaking desert nature and the magic of the Hoggar Mountains, which set it apart from the rest of the country. It is also home to the Tuareg tribes, who preserve their ancient traditions.

Top tourist places to visit in Tamanrasset:

  • Hoggar Mountains: One of the most famous mountain landmarks in Algeria, offering stunning natural scenery and a destination for adventurers and climbers.
  • Assekrem: A high point that offers one of the most beautiful sunset views in the world.
  • Old City of Tamanrasset: Where you can explore Tuareg culture and traditional markets.
  • The Black Valley (The Deep Valley): A fascinating geological area characterized by its black volcanic rocks.
  • Sbeiba Festival (Djanet): Held annually, it showcases Tuareg traditions through music, clothing, and food.

12 - Tebessa

Located in the far east of Algeria, Tebessa is one of the oldest historical regions in the country. This province is distinguished by its ancient archaeological heritage dating back to the Roman era, along with its natural landscapes and local traditions.

Top tourist places to visit in Tebessa:

  • Roman Theater: One of the most important well-preserved Roman theaters in Algeria, reflecting the city's ancient roots.
  • Roman Gate (Arch of Caracalla): One of the most famous historical monuments dating back to the Roman period.
  • Mount Bouzghaza: Offers wonderful natural views and is a destination for climbing and hiking enthusiasts.
  • Regional Museum: Displays rare archaeological artifacts from various historical periods.
  • Oases and Agricultural Areas: Especially in the regions of Bekkaria and Negrine, where nature and tranquility meet.

13 - Tlemcen

Tlemcen is considered one of the most beautiful provinces in western Algeria, nicknamed the "Pearl of the Maghreb" thanks to its ancient history, Andalusian architectural arts, and the beauty of its diverse nature, including mountains and waterfalls.

Top tourist places to visit in Tlemcen:

  • Lalla Setti Plateau: A breathtaking viewpoint overlooking the mountains, attracting adventure and nature lovers.
  • The Kasbah and El Mechouar Palace: Historical landmarks featuring palaces and walls dating back to the Zianid era.
  • Sidi Boumediene Mosque: An Andalusian architectural masterpiece dating back to the 14th century.
  • Ain Fezza Caves: Enchanting natural caves and an ideal spot for exploration enthusiasts.
  • "Taffessit" Forest Tourism Area: A green mountainous area perfect for strolling and relaxation.
  • Honaïne Beach: A beautiful coastal destination in the west of the province.

14 - Tiaret

Located in the western High Plateaus, Tiaret Province is distinguished by its ancient history dating back to antiquity. It was once known as "Tahert" and served as the capital of the Rustamid dynasty. It also boasts stunning natural landscapes and vast plains, making it a prominent agricultural region.

Top tourist places to visit in Tiaret:

  • Frenda Archaeological Site: Ancient Roman ruins that reflect the rich history of the region.
  • Mount Sougeur: A favorite destination for hiking and mountain climbing enthusiasts.
  • The Jeddars (Tombs of Numidian Kings): Ancient pyramidal funeral monuments, among the oldest archaeological landmarks in Algeria.
  • Madroussa Forest: An ideal green area for family trips and relaxation.
  • Mina Waterfalls: A magnificent natural scene and a preferred spot for nature and tranquility lovers.

15 - Tizi Ouzou

Located in the Greater Kabylie region, Tizi Ouzou Province is considered one of Algeria's most beautiful provinces due to its breathtaking nature and rich cultural heritage. It is famous for its stunning mountains, hospitable people, and traditional crafts, especially Kabyle dresses and silver jewelry.

Top tourist places to visit in Tizi Ouzou:

  • Ibakwaren Village: One of the most beautiful traditional villages in the Kabylie region with scenic mountain views.
  • Akfadou Forest: A dense forest and an excellent destination for nature trekking.
  • Djurdjura Mountains: One of Algeria's highest ranges, covered in snow during winter, offering magical landscapes.
  • Beni Yenni (Aït Yenni): Famous for traditional silver jewelry making and local folk markets.
  • Aït Irane Waterfalls: One of the most prominent natural landmarks in the region.

16 - Algiers

Algiers is the political and administrative capital of Algeria and the beating heart of the country in terms of economy, culture, and history. It combines a modern character with historical landmarks that reflect the diverse civilizations that have passed through it.

Top tourist places to visit in Algiers:

  • The Casbah: The ancient historic city, a UNESCO World Heritage site, featuring traditional houses and old mosques.
  • Jardin d'Essai du Hamma: A historic botanical garden, ranked among the world's most beautiful, containing thousands of plant species.
  • Martyrs' Memorial (Maqam Echahid): A massive monument commemorating the martyrs of the revolution, offering a panoramic view of the capital.
  • The Great Mosque of Algiers: The third-largest mosque in the world, distinguished by its modern design and soaring minaret.
  • Museum of Fine Arts: Houses collections of paintings and sculptures from various eras.
  • Bardo National Museum: Displays rare artifacts from prehistory to the Islamic era.
  • Sidi Fredj: A coastal tourist area featuring hotels, a marina, and recreational facilities.
  • Palm Beach: One of the capital's most beautiful beaches, suitable for families and visitors.
  • Palais des Rais (Bastion 23): A historical landmark overlooking the sea, showcasing Ottoman architecture.

17 - Djelfa

Djelfa Province is one of the most important provinces of the High Plateaus, distinguished by its strategic location between the north and south. This gives it a diverse climate and attractive terrain that combines plains, mountains, and desert. Djelfa is also famous for its authentic Bedouin culture, traditional markets, and the legendary hospitality of its people.

Top tourist places to visit in Djelfa:

  • Zaccar Archaeological Area: An archaeological site containing rock engravings dating back to prehistoric times.
  • Mount Sen Albahr: One of the highest peaks in the province, offering a great spot for adventure and nature lovers.
  • Moulaba Forest: A breathtaking natural space ideal for strolling and relaxation, especially during spring and autumn.
  • Wadi Mellah: A natural tourist area that attracts visitors with its green mountainous nature.
  • Ain Azal: An area famous for its water springs and ecological diversity.
  • "Touiza" Tradition: A unique local folk tradition that reflects the spirit of solidarity among the region's inhabitants.

18 - Jijel

Jijel Province is considered one of the most beautiful coastal provinces in Algeria, characterized by its stunning nature that combines the sea, mountains, and forests. Located on the eastern coast, it is famous for its beautiful beaches and pure air, making it a preferred destination for summer and family tourism.

Top tourist places to visit in Jijel:

  • The Marvelous Caves (Les Grottes Merveilleuses): A formation of natural caves with strange shapes, considered one of the most prominent landmarks in the province.
  • Jijel Corniche: A magnificent coastal road offering breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Jijel Fishing Port: A vibrant place reflecting the city's maritime heritage and offering a unique experience for visitors.
  • Andre Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in the province, characterized by its clear waters and golden sands.
  • Ziama Mansouriah Region: A mountainous and coastal area at the same time, ideal for lovers of nature and tranquility.

19 - Setif

Setif Province is regarded as one of the most important provinces in Algeria economically and culturally, and it is nicknamed the "Capital of the High Plateaus." Located in the east of the country, it features diverse terrain between mountains and plains, as well as a relatively moderate climate and a lively atmosphere.

Top tourist places to visit in Setif:

  • Djemila Archaeological Site (Cuicul): An ancient Roman city classified as a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most prominent historical landmarks in Algeria.
  • Ain El Fouara Fountain: The iconic landmark in the heart of Setif city center.
  • Beni Aziz Waterfalls: Enchanting natural waterfalls that attract visitors during the spring season.
  • Park Mall and City Center: Known for its modern facades and commercial activity.
  • Mount Babor Resort: A wonderful mountainous area for skiing and adventure lovers during the winter season.

20 - Saida

Saida Province is located in western Algeria and is known for its stunning nature and mountains that extend over a wide area, in addition to its famous mineral springs. It is considered one of the quiet provinces suitable for nature and relaxation lovers.

Top tourist places in Saida Province:

  • Hammam Rabi: One of the most famous mineral baths in Algeria, distinguished by its therapeutic sulfurous waters, attracting visitors from various provinces.
  • Ain Skhouna Forest: A charming natural area rich in pine trees, suitable for camping and outings.
  • Tafna Valley: One of the important valleys in the region, forming a beautiful natural scene, especially during the rainy season.
  • Regional Museum: Displays artifacts and belongings reflecting the region's ancient history and heritage.

21 - Skikda

Skikda Province is located in northeastern Algeria, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, making it one of the country's most important coastal destinations. It is famous for its long shorelines, lush mountainous nature, and ancient historical sites.

Top tourist places in Skikda Province:

  • Larbi Ben M'hidi Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Algeria, featuring golden sands and clear waters.
  • Cap de Fer: A famous natural landmark, a soaring rocky cape overlooking the sea with breathtaking views.
  • The Old Town: Contains buildings dating back to the colonial era and narrow streets filled with authenticity.
  • Beni Bechir Forest: A great destination for nature lovers and hiking in quiet green paths.
  • Beni Diab Cave: One of the interesting natural caves that attract visitors to explore its rock formations.

22 - Sidi Bel Abbes

Sidi Bel Abbes Province is located in western Algeria and is considered a major agricultural and industrial hub. It features diverse natural landscapes combining green plains and hills, and is famous for its deep-rooted culture and authentic folk heritage.

Top tourist places in Sidi Bel Abbes Province:

  • Sfifef Dam: A wonderful place to relax in nature and enjoy the calm waters and scenery.
  • Tessala Mountain: A favorite destination for climbing and nature enthusiasts, offering stunning views of the surrounding areas.
  • The Public Garden (City Center): An ideal spot for families, distinguished by its beauty and cleanliness.
  • Palace of Culture: Offers cultural and artistic performances reflecting the region's cultural diversity.
  • Traditional Folk Markets: Where visitors can discover traditional crafts and local products.

23 - Annaba

Annaba Province is located in northeastern Algeria and is one of the most beautiful coastal cities in the country. Known as the "Pearl of Eastern Algeria," it boasts breathtaking nature and charming beaches. It is also a historical region with Phoenician and Roman roots.

Top tourist places in Annaba Province:

  • Refas Zahouane Beach: One of Algeria's most beautiful beaches, known for its cleanliness and golden sands.
  • Saint Augustine Basilica: A religious and historical landmark dating back to the French period, situated on a hill overlooking the city.
  • The Old Town (La Place): A popular neighborhood reflecting the city's authenticity and social heritage.
  • Edough Mountains: A destination for nature lovers and hiking through green forests.
  • Hippo Regius Roman Ruins: Remains of an ancient Roman city featuring a theater, forums, and archaeological columns.

24 - Guelma

Guelma Province is located in northeastern Algeria, famous for its green nature and hot mineral springs. It is a beloved destination for natural therapy and mountain tourism, as well as being rich in Roman archaeological sites.

Top tourist places in Guelma Province:

  • Hammam Debagh: One of Algeria's most famous mineral baths, known for its healing properties and hot waterfalls.
  • Roman Ruins: Scattered across several areas, including Khamissa and M'daourouch, featuring theaters and stone columns.
  • Ain Sefra Forest: A breathtaking natural site suitable for outings and relaxation amidst greenery.
  • Bouhamdane Waterfalls: One of the most beautiful natural sites in the province, visited for relaxation and photography.
  • Tomb of Lalla Fatma N'Soumer: A historical landmark honoring one of the icons of Algerian resistance.

25 - Constantine

Constantine is nicknamed the "Capital of Eastern Algeria" and the "City of Hanging Bridges." It is one of the oldest cities in the country, distinguished by its thousands of years of history and unique terrain that makes it a city suspended over rocks and canyons.

Top tourist places in Constantine Province:

  • Sidi M'Cid Bridge: The city's most famous bridge and a symbol of Constantine, built over the Rhumel Gorge at a soaring height.
  • Ahmed Bey Palace: A magnificent Ottoman historical landmark, known for its architecture and authentic decorations.
  • The Deep Gorge: The Rhumel Valley that passes through the city center, forming a breathtaking backdrop.
  • Emir Abdelkader Mosque: One of Algeria's largest and most beautiful mosques, reflecting the splendor of Islamic architecture.
  • The Old Medina: With its narrow traditional markets like Souq El Acer, filled with life and history.
  • Cirta Museum: Displays rare artifacts from ancient civilizations, especially Numidian and Roman.

26 - Medea

Medea Province is located in northern Algeria and is one of the provinces that combines mountainous nature with ancient history. It is characterized by its temperate climate, beautiful highlands, and fertile plains, and it is rich in tourist and cultural landmarks.

Top tourist places in Medea Province:

  • Chréa National Park: Part of it lies in Medea and is considered one of the most beautiful relaxation areas in Algeria, especially in winter.
  • Tomb of Sultan Abderrahmane Al-Thaalibi: A historical and spiritual landmark that attracts visitors from various regions.
  • Ain Baïcha Cave: A stunning natural site located in the mountains, serving as a destination for exploration enthusiasts.
  • First of November Square: The vibrant heart of the city, surrounded by cafes and historical facilities.
  • Beni Slimane Region: Famous for producing traditional carpets and handmade textiles.
  • Ancient Palaces in Ammari: Historical ruins dating back to ancient periods representing traditional local architecture.

27 - Mostaganem

Mostaganem Province is located in northwestern Algeria, overlooking the western coast. It is known for its beautiful beaches, clean cities, and cultural diversity. The province combines history, nature, and rich folk heritage, making it a unique tourist destination.

Top tourist places in Mostaganem Province:

  • Cap Ivi: A magnificent coastal point with breathtaking views and pure waters, considered one of the best sites for swimming and relaxation.
  • Tidjdit Ancient Quarter: Reflects the old Andalusian character with narrow alleys and white houses.
  • "Mostaland" Zoo and Amusement Park: An excellent recreational facility for families, containing a zoo and a water park.
  • Abdelhamid Ben Badis Mosque: A religious and cultural landmark with a modern design.
  • Salamandre Corniche: A long tourist promenade overlooking the sea, featuring waterfront restaurants and cafes.
  • Mazagran and Kharouba Beaches: Some of the cleanest and most beautiful beaches in Algeria, suitable for swimming and water sports.

28 - M'Sila

M'Sila Province is located in the High Plateaus region of eastern Algeria and is distinguished by its ancient history and strategic location connecting the north and south. M'Sila combines Saharan character with natural beauty and hosts important archaeological sites.

Top tourist places in M'Sila Province:

  • Ancient City of Thabudeos: An ancient Roman site containing rare architectural ruins.
  • Al Qal'a of Beni Hammad: One of the most important UNESCO World Heritage sites; it was the first capital of the Hammadid empire and features a unique architectural style.
  • Mount Boukhil: A destination for climbing and mountain nature enthusiasts.
  • Koudiat Eddraou Dam: A suitable place for rest, outings, and watching quiet nature.
  • Traditional Markets in the City Center: Reflect the region's culture and offer local and artisanal products.

29 - Mascara

Mascara Province is located in northwestern Algeria, enjoying a long history and diverse geography that combines mountains and plains. It is known as the birthplace of Emir Abdelkader, a symbol of Algerian resistance against French colonialism.

Top tourist places in Mascara Province:

  • Shrine of Emir Abdelkader: An important historical and cultural landmark reflecting the symbolism of the resistance.
  • Oued Tahta Waterfalls: A breathtaking natural scene and a favorite spot for visitors and nature lovers.
  • Mount El Gaâda: Offers a beautiful view and is a destination for hiking hobbyists.
  • Old Casbah of Mascara: Includes historical architectural remains and traditional markets.
  • Fergoug Dam: An ideal place for rest and enjoying the tranquility and green scenery.

30 - Ouargla

Ouargla Province is located in southeastern Algeria and is one of the major Saharan provinces that combines ancient history, traditional architecture, and stunning natural sites. Ouargla is famous for its charming desert character and for being an important economic center due to oil and gas.

Top tourist places in Ouargla Province:

  • Ancient Oases: Such as Rouissat and Sidi Khouiled oases, where palm trees and water harmonize in breathtaking landscapes.
  • Traditional Ksour (Palaces): Such as Ksar Temacine and Ksar Rouissat, which are wonderful examples of Saharan architecture.
  • Ouargla Museum: Displays the history of the region and the culture of its inhabitants through the ages.
  • Great Ouargla Desert: A destination for camping, Saharan tourism, and camel riding.
  • Traditional Markets: Where local products such as Saharan clothing, dates, and handicrafts are displayed.

31 - Oran

Oran Province is one of Algeria's most prominent and vibrant coastal regions. Known as "El Bahia" (The Radiant), it is a city that blends history, art, modernity, and nature, making it an ideal destination for domestic and international tourists.

Top tourist places in Oran Province:

  • Santa Cruz Fort: Overlooking the city and the sea from Mount Murdjadjo, it is considered one of the most magnificent historical landmarks in Algeria.
  • Mount Murdjadjo: Offers a stunning panoramic view of the city and features paths for hiking and photography.
  • Emir Abdelkader Landmark: A symbolic monument located on a highland in the city center.
  • The Waterfront (Front de Mer): A wonderful area for walking and recreation with cafes and restaurants overlooking the sea.
  • Sidi El Houari District: The oldest neighborhood in Oran, containing Turkish baths and historical sites.
  • Abdelkader Alloula Theater: A French architectural masterpiece and a center for cultural life.
  • Sidi M'hamed Garden (Public Garden): A quiet place suitable for families and nature lovers.
  • Pasha Mosque: One of the oldest mosques, built in the 18th century in the Maghrebi architectural style.
  • Les Andalouses Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Algeria, visited for relaxation and water sports.
  • Mohamed Ben Ahmed Convention Center: A modern landmark hosting national and international exhibitions.
  • Ain El Turk Tourist Village: Resorts and hotels on the sea, a famous destination during the summer.

32 - El Bayadh

El Bayadh Province is located in southwestern Algeria and is characterized by its diverse terrain, ranging from plateaus to hills. Its ancient history combines authenticity with a quiet nature. It is considered a semi-arid province with a moderate Saharan character, rich in natural sites and cultural landmarks.

Top tourist places in El Bayadh Province:

  • The Ancient Ksar: One of the oldest traditional buildings reflecting the architectural character of the region.
  • Beni Ounif Ksar: An important historical site and a symbol of local identity.
  • Gour Mountains: A mountain range suitable for hiking and exploration, offering breathtaking views.
  • Brezina Oasis: Combines desert nature and water, an ideal destination for relaxation and photography.
  • Ain Melouk: One of the natural springs famous for its pure water and attractive surrounding scenery.
  • Tidjania Zawiya: An important religious and cultural landmark attracting visitors interested in Sufi orders.
  • Kef En-Naama: A charming natural spot characterized by rare plant and animal diversity.

33 - Illizi Province

Illizi Province is located in the extreme southeast of Algeria and is one of the largest provinces by area. It is distinguished by its stunning desert nature, sand dunes, and unique terrain that makes it an exciting destination for adventure and exploration lovers. It also houses prehistoric historical sites.

Top tourist places in Illizi Province:

  • Tassili n'Ajjer Plateau: A UNESCO World Heritage site containing thousands of rock drawings and engravings dating back thousands of years.
  • Djanet: A charming oasis in the middle of the desert, serving as the gateway to exploring the Sahara and the Tassili.
  • Ishine Desert: A natural tourist area with golden sand dunes and magical landscapes, especially at sunset.
  • Tassili Mountain Highlands: An ideal place for climbing and hiking amidst rare rock formations.
  • In Amenas: A quiet desert city and an industrial hub that also possesses breathtaking natural scenery.
  • Giant Sand Dunes: Unforgettable views for photography enthusiasts and desert adventure seekers.

34 - Bordj Bou Arreridj

Bordj Bou Arreridj Province is located in northeastern Algeria and is considered a link between the east and the center. It is characterized by its beautiful nature and diverse terrain, ranging from plains to mountains, in addition to its active economic life in industry and services.

Top tourist places in Bordj Bou Arreridj Province:

  • Bibans Mountains: A breathtaking mountain range suitable for outings and mountain climbing, especially in the spring.
  • Bouaziz Forest: A wonderful green space for nature and tranquility lovers.
  • Ain Zada Dam: A natural tourist destination for relaxation and fishing.
  • Ras El Oued: A city hosting ancient history and local heritage landmarks.
  • El M'hir Region: Famous for its traditional markets and cultural events.

35 - Boumerdes

Boumerdes Province is located on the northern coast of Algeria and is characterized by its natural beauty and tourist diversity. It combines the sea and mountains, making it an ideal destination for relaxation and adventure lovers. The province is also famous for its health resorts and mineral waters.

Top tourist places in Boumerdes Province:

  • Corso Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in the region, featuring golden sands and clear waters.
  • Hammam Thaalba: A traditional mineral resort that attracts visitors for treatment and relaxation.
  • Bouglelil Forest: An ideal place for nature lovers and family trips.
  • Zemmouri El Bahri Beach: A quiet beach suitable for swimming and summer outings.
  • Boumerdes City: The capital of the province, featuring a wonderful corniche, shopping areas, and entertainment venues.

36 - El Tarf

El Tarf Province is located in the extreme northeast of Algeria and is considered one of the most beautiful provinces in the country, thanks to its dense forests, calm lakes, and pristine coastline stretching along the Mediterranean Sea. It is characterized by its virgin nature, making it an ideal destination for nature and adventure lovers.

Top tourist places in El Tarf Province:

  • El Kala National Park: One of the most important nature reserves in Algeria, featuring a unique diversity of flora and fauna.
  • Lake Tonga: A nature reserve registered under the Ramsar Convention, home to many species of migratory birds.
  • Messida Beach: A quiet beach characterized by its natural beauty and tranquility, ideal for relaxation.
  • Virgin Forest: A dense green forest considered one of the best places for hiking in the province.
  • El Kala City: A beautiful coastal city famous for fishing and summer festivals.

37 - Tindouf

Tindouf Province is located in the extreme southwest of Algeria and is distinguished by its strategic location on the borders with Morocco and Mauritania. Known for its charming Saharan nature and ancient history, it is also famous for its oases and ancient palaces (ksour), serving as an important station on ancient caravan routes.
